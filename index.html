About

Camila Estrin Henriksen is a visual and textile artist with a focus on mark making, experimental textile art, and the materiality of paper. Her work explores how materials can bear and convey experiences beyond language. She holds a Master’s degree in Art from the University of Agder, where she developed the project "Honest Skin," a material exploration of trauma and healing.

Artistic Practice

Honest Skin (2021)
Recycled tea bags sewn with polyester thread. This work materializes trauma and healing through tactile exploration.

In Heaven We Feed Each Other (2018-2019)
Video installation exploring collaboration and shared humanity.

Teaching

Camila has taught experimental drawing workshops and courses such as "Idéutvikling og eksperimenterende prosesser" at the University of Agder, emphasizing material exploration, process, and inclusivity. Her pedagogical approach fosters a hands-on, sensory-driven learning environment.

Lesbos Experience

The Lesbos experience highlighted the potential of relational aesthetics and participatory ethnography. It inspired the project In Heaven We Feed Each Other, a reflection on collaboration and interdependence, framed through shared meals and stories.
